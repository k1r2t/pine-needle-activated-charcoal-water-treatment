# Pine Needle Derived Activated Charcoal for Wastewater Treatment and Dye Removal

## Overview

Water pollution caused by industrial discharge, dyes, suspended solids, and organic contaminants is a growing environmental challenge worldwide. Conventional water treatment technologies are often expensive and may not be accessible in resource-limited regions.

This project focuses on the development of a low-cost and eco-friendly activated charcoal (AC) produced from pine needles and its application in wastewater purification and industrial dye removal.

The study investigates the adsorption efficiency of pine needle activated charcoal through physicochemical analysis of contaminated water before and after treatment.

---

## Background

Pine needles are abundant forest waste materials, particularly in Himalayan regions. Improper disposal of pine needles contributes to forest fires and environmental degradation.

Converting pine needles into activated charcoal offers:

* Sustainable waste management
* Value-added biomass utilization
* Low-cost water purification
* Eco-friendly adsorption technology

---

## Objectives

The objectives of this project were:

* Preparation of activated charcoal from pine needles.
* Chemical activation using KOH treatment.
* Carbonization under nitrogen atmosphere.
* Characterization of activated charcoal.
* Evaluation of wastewater treatment efficiency.
* Assessment of dye removal capability.
* Comparison with commercial activated charcoal.

## Methodology

### Step 1: Collection of Pine Needles

* Washing
* Drying
* Crushing

### Step 2: Chemical Activation

Pine needles were immersed in:

* KOH solution
* Duration: 24 hours

### Step 3: Carbonization

Conditions:

* Nitrogen atmosphere
* Heating rate: 10°C/min
* Final temperature: 800°C
* Holding time: 1 hour

### Step 4: Acid Washing

Activated carbon was treated with:

* 5 M HCl

followed by extensive washing using distilled water.

### Step 5: Drying and Storage

* Drying at 105°C
* Pulverization
* Storage in desiccator

---

## Results

### pH Improvement

| Sample                 | pH        |
| ---------------------- | --------- |
| Untreated Water        | 5.32      |
| Pine Needle AC Treated | 7.17      |
| WHO Standard           | 6.5 – 9.5 |

The activated charcoal successfully restored acidic water to a near-neutral pH.

---

### Reduction in Water Pollutants

| Parameter | Before Treatment | After Treatment |
| --------- | ---------------- | --------------- |
| COD       | 672              | 149             |
| BOD       | 73.32            | 26.5            |
| TSS       | 112              | 54              |
| TDS       | 576              | 282             |
| Hardness  | 56.26            | 15.82           |

Significant reductions were observed across all major water quality indicators.

---

### Turbidity Reduction

* Initial Turbidity: 0.92 NTU
* Final Turbidity: 0.342 NTU

Reduction Efficiency:

81.6%

---

### Dye Removal

Industrial wastewater samples collected from:

* Textile industries
* Pharmaceutical industries

were passed through the pine needle activated charcoal filter.

Results demonstrated successful adsorption and removal of dye contaminants, indicating the potential application of pine needle activated charcoal in industrial wastewater treatment.
